# What’s new in the treatment plan experience

Plain-language summary of **the big shifts** you will notice when building a plan, reviewing the client record, sending the share link, or opening the post-visit blueprint. (No backend or admin detail.)

---

## Plan layout: active plan, wishlist, and completed

Discussed treatments are organized under **three headings** everywhere that matters for day-to-day work: the **client view**, the **plan builder**, and the **shared plan** preview.

- **Treatment plan** — lines you are treating as active, scheduled, or “this visit / next visit” style commitments.
- **Wishlist** — ideas you are not committing to yet; they stay out of the main plan list so nothing reads like it is already booked.
- **Completed** — work already done (including visit-mode “built” style completion).

On **client cards**, treatment lines are easier to scan: **when** something is planned and **which area** you discussed can appear **on one line** with a clear separator.

The plan header can show **last updated** and short dates (for example **Jun 26**, no year) so you see freshness at a glance; completed lines can show **when** they were checked off.

---

## After the visit: blueprint chapters (including **Other procedures**)

The **post-visit blueprint** no longer collapses everything vague into one blob.

- **Other procedures** — if the plan lists **several** procedure types on that row (for example PRFM and Skinvive on one “other” line), the blueprint can produce **one chapter per procedure**, in plan order, so the patient story matches what you actually discussed.
- **Energy treatment** — same idea when **multiple** device or modality types appear in the product text: the blueprint can **split into separate sections** (for example different laser or light treatments) instead of one combined chapter.
- Where we have them, **longevity** and **downtime** can follow the **specific** procedure for those sub-chapters instead of only a generic “varies” line.

---

## **Other procedures** in the plan builder (detail that matches the room)

The plan builder treats **Other procedures** as a first-class path, not a catch-all text field.

- You choose **what** was discussed from your practice’s procedure list (and related options), so the chart and downstream views stay structured.
- For **PRFM-style injection** lines, you can record **where** you discussed treating (for example **under the eyes** vs **nasolabial folds**), so documentation lines up with the conversation.

---

## Sharing the plan

- The **patient-facing** view uses the **same section story** (active plan vs wishlist vs completed) so nothing is ambiguous after they leave.
- Before you send, you can **adjust dollar amounts on multiple lines in one place** when you need a last-minute quote polish, instead of opening each line one by one.

---

## Checkout and estimates

**Checkout and line totals** use the **same price list your practice is tied to** in the dashboard, so what you quote in the tool matches that location’s book—not a generic sheet from another brand.

---

*Out of scope here: smaller UI polish (search quirks, chip spacing on phones), individual SKU renames unless your team asks for a catalog changelog, separate wellness-only catalogs, clinic-only login or pricing books, and admin or security work that does not change how you edit or share the plan day to day.*
